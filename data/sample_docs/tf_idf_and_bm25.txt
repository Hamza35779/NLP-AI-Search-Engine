TF-IDF stands for term frequency - inverse document frequency. It weights
each term in a document by how often it appears locally and how rare it
is across the whole collection. The resulting sparse vector representation
allows queries and documents to be compared with cosine similarity, where
documents that share many rare terms with the query rise to the top.

BM25, also known as Okapi BM25, is a probabilistic ranking function that
extends TF-IDF with two important refinements. First, it saturates the
contribution of repeated terms using a parameter k1, so a word appearing
ten times does not score ten times higher than appearing once. Second,
it normalizes for document length using a parameter b, so very long
documents do not unfairly dominate the ranking.

Both models are unsupervised and remarkably strong baselines. They form
the sparse retrieval component of many production search systems and are
often combined with dense retrievers for state-of-the-art quality.

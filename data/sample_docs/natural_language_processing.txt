Natural language processing, or NLP, is the field of artificial
intelligence that gives computers the ability to read, understand, and
generate human language. NLP combines linguistics, statistics, and
machine learning to build systems for translation, sentiment analysis,
question answering, summarization, and speech recognition.

Foundational tasks include tokenization, part-of-speech tagging, named
entity recognition, parsing, and word-sense disambiguation. Higher-level
tasks include machine translation, text classification, semantic role
labeling, coreference resolution, and dialogue systems.

Modern NLP is dominated by transformer-based language models such as
BERT, GPT, and T5. These models are pre-trained on massive text corpora
with self-supervised objectives and then fine-tuned for downstream tasks.
Retrieval-augmented generation combines a search engine with a language
model so the model can ground its answers in a document collection.

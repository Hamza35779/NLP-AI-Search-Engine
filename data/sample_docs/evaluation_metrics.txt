Evaluation metrics for ranked retrieval measure how well a system orders
relevant documents above non-relevant ones. Precision at k counts the
fraction of the top k results that are relevant, and recall at k counts
the fraction of all relevant documents that appear in the top k.

Average precision averages precision values at the ranks where relevant
documents appear, which rewards systems that place relevant documents
near the top of the list. Mean average precision, or MAP, averages this
quantity over a set of test queries and is one of the most widely used
single-number summaries of retrieval quality.

Mean reciprocal rank focuses on the rank of the first relevant document
and is well suited to known-item search, where each query has a single
correct answer. Normalized discounted cumulative gain, or nDCG, uses
graded relevance judgements and a logarithmic position discount, which
makes it the standard choice for web search evaluations.

Information retrieval (IR) is the science of searching for information in
documents, searching for documents themselves, searching for metadata that
describes documents, and searching within databases for text or sounds or
images. The classical IR problem is the ad-hoc query: a user describes
what they need with a short query, and the system ranks every document in
the collection by likely relevance.

The standard pipeline includes tokenization, normalization, stemming or
lemmatization, building an inverted index, and using a ranking model such
as TF-IDF or BM25 to score documents against queries. Modern systems also
incorporate learning-to-rank, dense retrieval with neural embeddings, and
hybrid approaches that combine sparse and dense signals.

Evaluation uses metrics such as precision, recall, mean average precision,
mean reciprocal rank, and normalized discounted cumulative gain. Test
collections like TREC, MS MARCO, and BEIR provide standard benchmarks for
comparing different retrieval systems.
